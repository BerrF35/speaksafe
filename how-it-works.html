How SpeakSafe Works

A step-by-step breakdown of the acoustic forensics pipeline that powers SpeakSafe's detection.

1
Audio Ingestion & Preprocessing
Your audio file is decoded into a raw PCM waveform and normalized to 16 kHz mono. We strip any container metadata (ID3 tags, comments) before processing. Files are never stored — they are processed in-memory and discarded after analysis.
16 kHz resample PCM decode Mono normalize WAV · MP3 · OGG · FLAC · M4A
2
Acoustic Feature Extraction
We compute 12 acoustic features across 25ms analysis windows with 10ms hop size. The key features include Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients (MFCCs), spectral centroid, zero-crossing rate, short-time energy, harmonic-to-noise ratio, and RMS uniformity. Each feature is measured for both its mean value and variance over time, because AI voices tend to be unnaturally consistent.
MFCC (13 coefficients) Spectral centroid ZCR HNR RMS energy Pitch F0
3
AI Classification Model
The extracted feature vectors are fed into a gradient-boosted ensemble trained on over 50,000 audio samples — half human speech, half AI-generated from 20+ TTS systems. The model outputs a probability score (0–100%) representing confidence in AI origin. A secondary model checks for hybrid content by analyzing temporal consistency across segments.
Gradient boost ensemble 50k+ training samples Segment-level analysis Hybrid detection
4
Results & Explanation
The result is returned as a structured JSON object with an AI probability score, classification label (Human / AI / Hybrid), likely generator attribution, per-signal breakdown, and natural-language explanations of what the model detected. A visual spectrogram is rendered from the audio showing frequency patterns over time.
AI probability % Generator attribution Spectrogram visualization Signal breakdown
Signal Features

What We Actually Measure

Human voices are chaotic in specific, measurable ways. AI voices are smooth in ways that betray them.

〰️
MFCC Variance
Human speech has natural micro-variation in cepstral coefficients. AI voices show unnaturally smooth, low-variance MFCC trajectories.
📊
Spectral Centroid Drift
The center of mass of the frequency spectrum drifts naturally in human speech. AI audio often exhibits predictable, cyclic drift patterns.
🔄
ZCR Periodicity
Zero-crossing rate — how often the waveform crosses zero — is stochastic in humans. Regular ZCR periodicity is a strong AI marker.
🎵
Harmonic Structure
AI vocoders often produce overly clean harmonic series. Real voices have natural jitter, shimmer, and slight inharmonicity.
📉
RMS Uniformity
Root Mean Square energy across time is too uniform in AI audio. Human speech has natural dynamic variation and micro-silences.
🌊
Frequency Banding
Neural TTS systems leave periodic horizontal artifacts in spectrograms. These frequency bands are a direct fingerprint of the synthesis process.
Accuracy & Limitations

SpeakSafe achieves ~91% accuracy on clean audio from known AI systems. Accuracy decreases for heavily compressed audio (e.g. <64 kbps MP3), audio shorter than 2 seconds, or custom fine-tuned models not in our training set.

This tool is intended as forensic evidence support, not definitive proof. Do not rely solely on SpeakSafe for legal or consequential decisions.
